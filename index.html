What is BuyerGrid, and what does it actually do? +

BuyerGrid is a network of 3,378 Facebook real estate groups focused on Telugu-speaking property buyers. Builders, developers, and brokers promote their projects through pinned posts, banner placements, and launch packages across these groups. Your creative reaches active buyers who are already in the market — not a broad audience that needs to be warmed up.

Are these real groups? How do we verify the member counts? +

Yes. Every group is a real Facebook group with a public or semi-public member count. The figures on this page — 3,378 groups, 29.7 lakh total members, 5.3 lakh NRI buyers — are drawn from actual group data. The top groups (Hyderabad RE Investors: 2,54,200 members; NRIs Indian People RE Investors: 1,15,800 members; etc.) can be verified directly by searching the group name on Facebook.

How does the NRI audience work? Why do they matter for builders? +

BuyerGrid includes 5.3 lakh NRI and overseas Telugu buyers in its network — concentrated in groups for Telugu communities in the US, UK, Australia, Singapore, UAE, and Gulf. These are high-net-worth investment buyers who are actively looking to invest in Indian real estate, especially Hyderabad and AP. For builders selling flats above ₹50 lakh, this audience is disproportionately valuable and extremely hard to reach through conventional advertising.

What is the payment process and when does my campaign go live? +

Payment is via UPI or bank transfer. Once payment is confirmed, your campaign goes live the same day. You receive screenshot proof of every pinned post and banner placement within 24 hours — showing your creative live in each group.

Can BuyerGrid design the banner or post creative? +

Yes. Creative design is available as an add-on for ₹2,000. Just mention it when you WhatsApp. If you already have your own banner or post graphic, simply send it over — any standard size works.

Which cities and markets does BuyerGrid cover? +

Core coverage is Hyderabad and Telangana (~17 lakh members), including Miyapur, Madhapur, Kukatpally, Hitech City, Karimnagar, Warangal, Shadnagar, Nizamabad, and Sangareddy. What is the difference between a pinned post and a banner? +

A pinned post is your promotional post pinned to the top of the group — the first thing every member sees when they open the group. It stays pinned for 7 days. A banner placement is your image shown in the group header or cover photo area for 30 days — persistent brand visibility every time someone visits the group.
