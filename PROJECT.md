## Part 2: The Strategic Position

### What makes Design Intend different (the real moat)

- **Founder-led, every project.** Ar. Chittrarasan personally leads every project. No client gets handed to a junior. In an industry where architects routinely overpromise then disappear, this is the single biggest trust signal.
- **Local roots, deep.** Chittrarasan studied in Hosur and stayed. He knows Hosur's soil (red laterite vs black cotton pockets), Hosur's elevation (3,000 ft, cooler than the plains), the DTCP approval process, local fabricators, Krishnagiri granite quarries. A city-based architect cannot match this.
- **Real Gensler experience.** Before founding Design Intend, Chittrarasan led interiors at Gensler Bengaluru for Chase Bank, Starbucks, and GMFI. This brings global-firm execution standards to a regional practice. Most Hosur architects cannot claim this.
- **Transparent published pricing.** ₹1,950 / ₹2,050 / ₹2,300 per sq.ft on the homepage. Most architects refuse to publish pricing.
- **Fixed-price contracts with milestone payments.** 10% on signing, 20% on foundation, and so on. No large advances. This protects the client and signals confidence.
- **Real testimonials with real names, locations, dates.** Kishore (Perithalmanna, Kerala, 2026), Vinutha Rajan (Bengaluru, 2024), Ambrish Suresh (Hosur, 2026), Rajesh Iyer (Plant Head, Jamna Auto Industries, Hosur, 2026). Verifiable.
- **COA registered.** Council of Architecture, India. Legal architectural practice with the right to sign building approval drawings.
- **Honest about scope.** "We are probably not the right studio for the cheapest possible drawings with no follow-up." This kind of honesty filters in the right clients.

### The target customer

**Primary (60% of work):**
- Families building their first home in Hosur, Krishnagiri district, or surrounding areas
- Budget: ₹50 lakh to ₹2 crore
- Plot size: 1,500 to 4,000 sq.ft built-up area
- Building once. Wants to get it right. Lives in Hosur, Bengaluru, or nearby. May be on first or second salary in tech/manufacturing/automotive.

**Secondary (25%):**
- High-net-worth families building villas or farmhouses
- Budget: ₹2 crore to ₹10 crore+
- Often plots in Bengaluru outskirts (Sarjapur, Attibele), Krishnagiri district, or Kerala (Perithalmanna and similar towns)
- Wants something distinctive, not a standard plan

**Tertiary (15%):**
- Manufacturing companies and corporates needing office or factory interiors
- Project size: 5,000 to 20,000+ sq ft
- Concentrated in Hosur's industrial belt
- Jamna Auto Industries (12,000 sq ft, 2026) is the lighthouse reference

### The buyer's question (the master question every page should serve)

*"Can this architect actually deliver my project — on time, on budget, and to the quality I am hoping for?"*

Every page on the site is some version of an answer to that question. Trust is the conversion lever, not cleverness. Photos, credentials, real client stories, specific local knowledge, and transparent pricing all serve trust. Marketing fluff destroys it.

### Architecture-specific vocabulary to use correctly

Use these naturally where appropriate. Misuse damages credibility with informed buyers.

- **Built-up area (BUA)** vs **carpet area** vs **super built-up area** — know the difference and use correctly
- **DTCP** — Directorate of Town and Country Planning (Tamil Nadu)
- **CMDA, BBMP, BDA** — for Bengaluru references
- **Setback** — distance between building and plot boundary
- **FSI / FAR** — Floor Space Index / Floor Area Ratio
- **Massing** — the overall three-dimensional form of a building
- **Vernacular architecture** — design traditions specific to a region
- **Jaali** — perforated screen, traditional Indian architecture
- **Vastu** — traditional Indian architectural principles (use respectfully, neither endorse nor dismiss)
- **Brick jaali, terrazzo, lime-wash, laterite, teak joinery** — real materials, use specifically
- **Joinery** not "carpentry work" (in context of architecture)
- **Fenestration** for windows and openings when speaking technically

### Required writing patterns (specific to architecture)

- Specific materials. "Italian marble" not "premium stone." "Jaquar fittings" not "branded sanitary ware."
- Specific dimensions. "3,800 sq ft duplex" not "spacious home."
- Specific timelines. "10 months from brief to handover" not "quickly delivered."
- Specific locations. "Perithalmanna, Kerala" not "a beautiful coastal town."
- Specific people. "Family of five" not "a family." "Plant Head at Jamna Auto" not "a corporate client."

## Part 6: Service and Project Page Rules

Architecture pages have different rules than consulting pages. Buyers are evaluating a 12-month decision worth lakhs to crores. Trust signals dominate.

### Every service page must have

1. **Photo first.** A real project photo (Design Intend's own work) above the fold.
2. **Specific pricing where applicable.** ₹1,950-₹2,300 per sq.ft for construction. Interior pricing per project type.
3. **Specific timeline.** "10-12 months" not "completed quickly."
4. **At least 2 named project references.** With location, year, and key detail.
5. **Specific local knowledge.** Something only a Hosur-experienced architect would know.
6. **Process explained.** What happens after the client calls.
7. **What is NOT included.** Land cost, government approvals, water/electricity connections, etc. Transparency.
8. **WhatsApp CTA at the top and bottom.** This is how most Indian residential leads come in.
9. **Phone number prominently.** +91 73976 06382.
10. **At least one client testimonial relevant to that service.**
11. **At least 3 FAQ items at the bottom** answering real buyer objections.

### Every project page must have (when built)

1. **Hero photo.** Best exterior or signature interior shot.
2. **Project metadata block.** Location, year, area, budget tier, project type, duration.
3. **The brief.** What the client wanted, in one paragraph.
4. **The design response.** How we approached it, in 2-3 paragraphs.
5. **Photo gallery.** 8-15 images minimum, captioned.
6. **Floor plan or massing diagram** where releasable.
7. **Materials list.** Specific brands and types used.
8. **Client quote.** Real, attributed, with permission.
9. **Related projects.** Links to 2-3 similar Design Intend projects.
10. **CTA.** "Start your project" or "Talk to the architect."

### Things service and project pages must never do

- Use stock photos labelled as project work (use Unsplash images only as decorative section breaks, never as project work — and even then, prefer Design Intend's own photography)
